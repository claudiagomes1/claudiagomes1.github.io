Synthwave

Synthwave is a musical sub-genre of hauntology, characterised by being an homage to the music and aesthetics of the 1980s. It channels a romanticised nostalgia for carefree summers, American beaches, piers and arcades, with the omnipresence of technology, science fiction, neon lights and retro-futurism.

Sovietwave

Sovietwave is a musical sub-genre of synthwave dating from the 2010s. It is characterised by being a style that revisits Soviet culture and carries a sense of nostalgia for the 1980s USSR, a sense of nostalgia for a future that embraced progress and space, which never came to take place with the implementation of Perestroika.

Spacewave

Spacewave is a genre that derives from cinematic synthwave and features an ethereal theme. It focuses on producing for the listener a futuristic, dream-like environment, an escape from reality. Due to its power to shift the listener spatially, this genre is often used in the music production of science fiction films.

about

The project seeks to depict the relationship between three inherently linked musical genres - synthwave, sovietwave, spacewave - and interpret it visually through a symbolic journey between them. This special edition seeks to represent the sense of dissociation from the real world caused by nostalgia of a future that never existed, exercising the transition between physical and terrestrial space (RISE represents synthwave), to the process of travel itself (LIFT enacts sovietwave), which in turn forwards to an immaterial, remote and dispersed space (VOID as spacewave) as a result of this profound nostalgia. The musical genres and sub-genres incorporated in this special edition draw a curious parallel with today's generation. Whereas the latter were born out of a nostalgic sense of a future that never came to pass, Gen-z are drawn to be carrying a sense of exclusion, of misunderstanding, of relating to a present or a future that does not exist or has been taken away from them, as a whole. Born in the midst of a confluence of information and novelties, the sense of belonging is vague and confused, and eternally in metamorphosis.
